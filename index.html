AI-Assisted Teaching Resource

P5 Maths · WA1 Cheatsheet · 2025

The student

She's smart and catches on fast but falls short in English. When exam questions use tricky phrasing, she loses marks that have nothing to do with her actual ability. That felt unfair to me.

What I noticed

She needed to see things to understand them. One gap that kept coming up was knowing when to multiply versus divide. So I grounded it in something concrete: multiplication as repeated groups (3 sets of 2 cookies = 6, so 3 × 2 = 6), and division as splitting equally (6 cookies shared between 3 friends = 2 each, so 6 ÷ 3 = 2). Once she could picture it, it stuck.

Before the cheatsheet

We had been working through workbooks and assessment books together, but we ran out of questions. Rather than buying more books with questions that might not even match what she needed, I used AI to generate new ones tailored to the exact topics and question styles she would see in her exam. I also adjusted the numbers for variety so she was getting fresh practice each time. Saves time, saves money, and it's personalised to her.

Why a cheatsheet

With her WA1 coming up, I wanted her to be able to sit with a paper independently and actually finish it. That's something a lot of kids struggle with, not because they don't know the content, but because they panic when they get stuck. Having a reference to come back to helps. It's something uni students use all the time, and I thought: why not for her too?

How I made it

I sketched out the structure on my iPad first, mapping her weak areas to specific examples. Then I used AI to generate the cheatsheet from that, reviewed every page, and fixed errors (arrows pointing the wrong way, sizing issues).

On using AI

It took about 2 hours in total. Felt long at first, but doing it without AI would've meant figuring out layout, fonts, sizing and spacing from scratch, that alone would've been most of the day. AI handled the production. I just had to make sure the thinking behind it was right.
